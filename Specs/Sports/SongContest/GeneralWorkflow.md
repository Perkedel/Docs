### Startup Qualifications

Rule for songs to be used for contest as follows

- Song
  - Max One song per team!
  - Originality.
    - Must be written by the contestant be involved in a team
    - Cannot be from 3rd party commission that won't be included in stage
    - Cannot be from 3rd party commission coming from generation such as AI LLM
  - Parody.
    - Admit it. Entirely original song is haaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaard!!! Okeh, then you can either be
      - Licensed for Cover song. e.g. MIDI cover
      - Licensed edited parody
      - Copyright Expired song. You can pick up archived songs whose from author died over 70 years ago (which on Earth will turned into CC0), or around here in DNB where they died over 2 years ago (which again turned into CC0 if it's copyrighted & proprietary (anything contains NoCommercial, NoDerivatives), otherwise belongs to the next of kin or record label same open source royalty free license permanently).
  - Old.
    - Since forcing brand new original may turn such entry into manmade slops anyway, you can use your existing magnum opus you have.
    - Must never been contested in Mosfier Song Contest in any history before.
  - Time Length
    - Maximum 00:05:00 song length
    - Recommended length is usually 3 Minutes & 10 seconds sharp 
  - Content.
    - Even if you had to use MIDI GM1 (Doom MIDI), at least make it like you're composing for God, Collosian 3:23. Therefore
    - Audio to MIDI banned
    - Requires at least
      - Melody and/or Vocal
      - Lead
      - Bass
      - Drums
    - **Lyrics and Words optional!**. Your songs can be fully instrumental. What? Song Contest right? not Singing Contest!
    - **Must have MIDI cover if your main song is not based on MIDI file itself**. Songs made as FLP, Reaper, Ableton, LMMS, Musescore, Analog Synth Tape Record, anything that's not MIDI file.
      - You can use our Mosfier service to remake the MIDI file for you. You can still use the original audio file in the stage
    - Metronome Compositions. Told you, it's easier to just use MIDI file, coz it's basically Time Signaturings. E.g., if your song slows, and then back to normal, very important to know.
    - MIDI Metadata. Yamaha's Parameters, Roland's Parameters, Van Elektronische's Parameters, etc etc. MIDI file must contains
      - XF Lyrics alongside standard Lyrics. If your song is intrumental, must be filled it with `--- --- ---`s so the XF Chord does not compact in older Yamaha Keyboard Lyric viewer. You should find this pre-filler in your Heikoplate's PSRUTI
        - Cue informations like `INTRO`, `INTERLUDE`, and `ENDING` are mandatory.
      - XF Descriptions. Lyric viewer when song stoppped such as `Music by`, `Words by`, etc.
      - XF Chords. What chords to play if next time in a camp party somebody wanted to play guitar cover of your song?
      - Title Banner. Text that pops up at the top text segment part of the LCD, usually the title of song.
        - You can use this too for Lyrics, optional. Extra points
      - LCD both Yamaha's & Roland's. Usually song logo
        - You can use this for MV, optional. Extra points
      - Mainly formatted for latest high end Yamaha Keyboards there is such as `G2` (Genos 2). The compatibility formats (`XA`, `GM`, `XT1~5`, `KORG`, `GS`, etc.) are for later.
      - Motion cue. Data usually used for epic stage show, that'll trigger some machines in the play automatically. Here, it's used for lights, select video files, burst the flame, etc.
      - Sync 1 to 1 for MIDI vs. original Audio, tolerance of 0.05 ms. Because when performance, the MIDI file will be played into Octavia.
  - Disclosure.
    - List all members and instruments involved
    - Cannot use 3rd party member that will not exclusively works for the team. If you had to use the power, then you must obtain the copy of their power, including self hosting the power.
    - And the newly created member must also be disclosed and how do they work. Yeah, everyone must know if e.g. music was made by another Intelligent Construct of the same nation, and/or e.g. the team consists of kitbash Intelligent Constructs.
  - Copyright. 
    - Royalty Free. Songs that's validated into Base Qualification must be Royalty Free, Gratis, and Commercial use allowed.
    - License Form. Therefore, when you submitted song, you have another consent form you are required to sign, stating that you are consent for your songs be
      - Owned by Mosfier's Record Label, Author still whoever composed the song
      - Royalty Free. Claim system moved into Mosfier's or removed entirely.
      - Gratis to download
      - Commercial Use allowed
      - All above at least for usage in DNB's network.
- Assets. In stage performance you of course wanted to make props for it.
  - Royalty Free, Gratis, & Commercial use allowed. All CADs, STLs, Blends, GLTF, VRM, FBX, Mesh, Furry, PMX and Motions, 3D models, Game Engine Projects, All Renders for the stage screen & mixed reality, everything, are also included in the required license consent like your song be contested.
  - It is also recommended that you upload all of these schematics & other performed datas yourselves, best to atleast from Archive.org, GitHub, GitLab, Smutbase / Open3DLab, Odysee. Then anything else. Be sure to link back to our own provided repository as well in the description or pinned comment.

## Calamity

### Draw

In case of a tie, the next measurement are taken into considerations as follows

- Most Points in a history. Frequency counts from `12` (worths 12 breaks) all the way down to `1` (worths 1 breaks).
- Numbers of `0` Tiered Votes. Worths 20 breaks. You're welcome!

### Inability to show up

We can use hologram & mixed reality technology to replace you temporarily, coz we don't like cancelations. This is why we record your Main qualification performance and save it during the era.  
Look, we're talking about songs, not singing.

And because we based it all on Main Qualification, you really really really have to show up ultimately so that it's backed up. Otherwise your team will be disqualified, replaced with another compositions to continue.
